## Competitor Landscape

### Phase 1 Search Findings

**Search 1: "web design small business Scotland"**
- No major national paid ads observed (Wix/Squarespace/GoDaddy not prominent in organic either)
- Organic competition: 24webdesign.co.uk, kimscotland.com, hybridanchor.com, tigerchick.com, speedypancake.co.uk
- All **local Scottish agencies** — no national brands dominating paid positions on this specific term
- **Assessment:** Local agency competition present, but no deep-pocketed national players — moderate opportunity

**Search 2: "website design Scotland tradespeople"**
- Strong niche competition from trade-specialist national UK providers: tradepixels.co.uk, websites-4-trades.co.uk, trustedtradedesign.co.uk, builtfortrades.co.uk, webuildstores.co.uk
- Also local: westcoastwebsites.co.uk, pipedreamdigital.co.uk (Glasgow-focused)
- Some national specialists with dedicated trade landing pages — they may bid on this term
- **Assessment:** Moderate competition; Nith Digital's Scotland + affordable + no-upfront angle is a clear differentiator

**Search 3: "affordable website Scotland small business"**
- Results: jdwebdesigns.com, hybridanchor.com, edinburghwebsites.com, speedypancake.co.uk, soapboxdigital.co.uk
- All local Scottish agencies — no Wix/GoDaddy/Squarespace in results for this Scotland-specific variant
- **Assessment:** Low-moderate; good opportunity for Nith Digital

**Search 4: "business startup checklist Scotland"**
- Results dominated by **government/public sector**: findbusinesssupport.gov.scot, bgateway.com, mygov.scot, rbs.co.uk
- No commercial paid ads observed — this is an informational space owned by official bodies
- **Assessment:** Very low competition for paid ads; easy to stand out with the Launchpad free checklist offer
- Nith Digital's Launchpad is a direct competitor to these resources as a free tool — strong positioning

**Search 5: "register self employed Scotland checklist"**
- Results: gov.uk, mygov.scot, findbusinesssupport.gov.scot — all government sites
- No commercial advertisers; purely informational SERP
- **Assessment:** Very low competition; intent matches Launchpad perfectly (sole traders just starting out)

### Key Takeaways
- **Local Scottish agencies are the main competition** for web design terms — none have national advertising budgets
- **National trade specialists** (Trade Pixels, Websites 4 Trades, Trusted Trade Design) may bid on tradespeople terms — but their targeting is UK-wide, not Scotland-specific, reducing relevance score
- **Startup/checklist terms are entirely uncontested** commercially — government sites rank organically, no one is paying for these clicks
- **National platforms (Wix, GoDaddy, Squarespace) are NOT visible** on Scotland-specific or trade-specific terms — this validates the long-tail Scotland strategy

Ad Group 3 (Launchpad) is a lead magnet funnel, so even a 5% conversion to an email sign-up at £0.45/click is excellent economics upstream of a £40/mo sale.

**What to pause immediately if budget runs out mid-day:**
1. Ad Group 1 first — broadest, most expensive
2. Ad Group 4 second — moderate CPC
3. Never pause Ad Groups 2 or 3 — these are the best ROI

## Next Steps — Data to Feed Back After 2 Weeks

After running for 14 days, pull the following from Google Ads and feed back into this process:

### 1. Search Term Report (most important)
- Export all actual search queries that triggered your ads
- Identify **irrelevant queries** not caught by current negatives → add to negative keyword list
- Identify **new keyword ideas** you hadn't thought of (e.g. "website for Scottish plumber" vs "plumber website Scotland")

### 2. Performance by Keyword
- Which keywords got clicks? Which got zero impressions? (Zero impressions = too low volume or bid too low)
- Click-through rate (CTR) by keyword — below 2% CTR suggests the ad copy doesn't match the keyword intent
- Which keywords produced form submissions or Launchpad sign-ups? (Set up conversion tracking in GA4 before launch)

### 3. Impression Share
- If impression share < 50% on your best keywords, raise the CPC cap slightly
- If impression share > 80% with high CTR → increase budget allocation to that ad group

### 4. Device performance
- Check mobile vs desktop split — if mobile CTR is high but conversion is low, consider mobile-specific bid adjustments (-20%)

### 5. Geographic report
- Where in Scotland are clicks coming from?
- If clicks cluster in Glasgow/Edinburgh, consider whether the ad copy is compelling enough vs. local Glasgow/Edinburgh agencies
- If strong performance from Highlands/rural Scotland → increase geographic bid for those regions

### 6. Launchpad funnel
- Track: clicks on Ad Group 3 → Launchpad page visits → checklist downloads/sign-ups → Startup Bundle enquiries
- If Launchpad sign-up rate < 15%, the landing page headline/CTA needs A/B testing
- Target: each Startup Bundle sale (£40/mo × 12 months = £480 LTV) should cost no more than £30 in ad spend to acquire

### 7. Quality Score check
- Pull quality scores for all keywords after 7 days
- Scores below 5/10 = landing page or ad copy relevance problem → fix before increasing budget
- Improve: add keyword in H1 of landing page, match ad copy language to the page headline
