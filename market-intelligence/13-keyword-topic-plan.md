## Overview

This plan covers 40 content topics across four categories, targeting local D&G search traffic, national long-tail keywords, comparison queries, and local interest content. Priority scoring (1–10) is based on search volume potential, relevance to Nith Digital services, and competitive opportunity (noting that local competitors publish almost no original content — a significant gap to exploit).

## Notes on Prioritisation

**Highest priority content (score 9–10):** Posts 1–5 in Section A are the cornerstone pieces — they directly address the buyer journey from "do I need a website?" through to "I want to find a local designer." These should be published first.

**Quick wins:** Google Business Profile content (Sections A #4, B #2) targets near-zero competition locally and has high intent from business owners actively seeking help.

**Differentiation through transparency:** The pricing post (Section A #2) and comparison posts (Section C) exploit the major gap competitors leave by never publishing their prices or honest comparisons.

**Local interest content:** Section D builds community goodwill and earns natural backlinks from local news sites, tourism bodies, and business organisations. Castle Douglas and Wigtown topics have real PR potential (local press coverage, social shares).
