## Category 1: Hotels (40–60 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
Hotels in D&G split into two camps:
1. **Larger/chain-affiliated properties** (Cally Palace via Bespoke Hotels, Fernhill via Bespoke Hotels, Knockinaam Lodge, Cairndale) — have professionally built WordPress or custom sites with booking integrations. Quality 7–8/10. Not ideal Nith Digital prospects unless seeking SEO improvement.
2. **Independent mid-size and small hotels** — built on outdated platforms (Joomla, early WordPress, proprietary builders), minimal SEO, poor photography, no conversion optimisation. Quality typically 4–6/10. Strong prospects.

### What's Missing Across the Sector
- SEO content targeting "hotel near Galloway Forest Park", "dog-friendly hotel Dumfries", etc.
- Speed/performance optimisation
- Direct booking incentives (most rely on Booking.com, paying 15% commission)
- Review integration on-site
- Events/weddings landing pages
- Local experience guides (upsells)

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** SEO package + website performance audit + direct booking optimisation
- **Key message:** "Every booking you get direct instead of via Booking.com saves you 15–25%. We'll help you get more direct bookings."
- **Price point:** £1,500–£3,500 for initial build/redesign; £300–£500/month SEO retainer

## Category 2: B&Bs and Guest Houses (180–250 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
This is one of the weakest digital categories in D&G. Research across Portpatrick, Dumfries, Castle Douglas and Kirkcudbright shows:
- ~40% have their own website — but majority are on legacy builders (BaseKit, older WordPress themes, Weebly)
- ~30% are Facebook-only
- ~30% rely solely on Booking.com, Airbnb, or VisitScotland listings
- Almost none have online booking functionality integrated on their own site

Typical site quality: 4–6/10. Sites are often slow, not mobile-optimised, and have no SEO. Many are last updated 3–5 years ago.

### What's Missing
- Mobile-responsive design
- Online booking integration (even a simple FreeToBook or SuperControl integration)
- SEO for "B&B [town name]" keywords (low competition, high intent)
- Google Business Profile optimisation
- Gallery with quality photography
- Reviews integration

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** New website build (starter package) + Google Business Profile setup + basic SEO
- **Key message:** "You're losing bookings to properties that are easier to find and easier to book. Let us fix that in 4 weeks."
- **Price point:** £800–£1,800 for a new site; optional £150/month maintenance

## Category 3: Holiday Cottages & Self-Catering (200–350 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
Wide variation:
- Estate-managed properties (Drumlanrig, Orroland, Kirkennan) have reasonable-to-good websites
- Independent owner-managed cottages are often poor — many are listed on Airbnb/VRBO/Sykes Cottages only, with no direct booking capability
- A significant number have no website at all; some have outdated sites with no booking calendar
- The real opportunity: owners paying 20–30% commission to Airbnb/Sykes on every booking

### What's Missing
- Direct booking engine (OwnerDirect, Freetobook, Hostaway, or Smoobu integration)
- SEO for "self-catering [location]", "dog-friendly cottage Galloway", etc.
- High-quality photography and virtual tours
- Email capture for repeat bookings
- Local area guides (improve SEO + guest experience)

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Website with integrated booking calendar + SEO
- **Key message:** "Airbnb charges you 3% per booking. Sykes charges you 25–35%. Your own site pays for itself in one or two redirected bookings."
- **Price point:** £1,200–£2,500 for a clean self-catering site with booking integration

## Category 4: Restaurants & Cafes (150–200 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
- Independent restaurants in Dumfries town (Palmerston Cafe, Abbey Cottage Tearoom, Boathouse Glencaple) have basic WordPress sites: quality 5–7/10
- Smaller cafes in Castle Douglas, Kirkcudbright, Newton Stewart typically have no website or a Facebook page only
- Almost none have integrated online booking — relying on phone or walk-in
- Menu PDFs common but not SEO-friendly
- Google My Business profiles often unclaimed or incomplete

### What's Missing
- Online booking for afternoon tea, Sunday lunch, special events
- Online ordering / click-and-collect
- Menu as web content (not a PDF) for SEO
- Events and private dining landing pages
- Email newsletter capture

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** WordPress site with menu pages + OpenTable/ResDiary booking integration + Google My Business management
- **Key message:** "A well-ranked Google listing for 'best cafe Dumfries' gets 200–400 searches per month. You should be first."
- **Price point:** £900–£2,000 for a food & drink site

## Category 5: Tradespeople (400–600 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
This is the single largest untapped category in D&G. Research found:
- Majority of sole traders and small firms are Facebook-only or listed on MyBuilder/Checkatrade only
- Those with websites often have extremely dated, non-mobile sites (like Ian Lewis Plumber — quality 3/10)
- Some have decent WordPress sites (RTL Plumbing, Stevie Wight Electrical) but lack SEO, testimonials, portfolio photos
- None discovered had a quote-request funnel or job lead capture beyond a phone number

### What's Missing
- Mobile-responsive website
- Google Business Profile optimisation
- Clear services + service area pages (critical for local SEO)
- Photo galleries of work completed
- Online quote/enquiry form
- Review integration

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Starter trade website (5 pages) + Google Business Profile + basic local SEO
- **Key message:** "When someone searches 'plumber Dumfries' at 9pm on a Sunday, your phone number on a Facebook page won't win the job. A website will."
- **Price point:** £600–£1,200 for a trade site; this segment is volume-sensitive so a productised package at £695–£995 would move well

## Category 6: Farm Shops & Food Producers (40–60 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
- Top operators (Kilnford, Barnbarroch Organic) have reasonable e-commerce sites (WordPress/WooCommerce, Shopify)
- Mid-tier operators (Loch Arthur Creamery Shop) have basic WooCommerce sites with thin product range
- Smaller farm shops and producers often have no website or a single-page brochure site
- Very few do any SEO or email marketing

### What's Missing
- E-commerce with local delivery / click-and-collect
- Subscription box functionality
- SEO for "local beef delivery Scotland" type terms
- Content marketing (recipes, farming stories, provenance articles)
- Social media integration

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** WooCommerce website with delivery zones + content strategy
- **Key message:** "D&G food is world class. Your website should let people outside the region order it."
- **Price point:** £1,500–£3,500 for an e-commerce food site

## Category 7: Estate Agents (15–25 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
- Most have a website but quality is typically 4–6/10
- GM Thomson (oldest, founded 1953) has a particularly weak site — ASP-based, not mobile-friendly, quality 4/10
- Williamson & Henry, Primrose & Gordon, Pollock & McLean have acceptable WordPress sites but lack modern features
- Threave Rural (land/rural specialist) has a clean enough site but minimal content and outdated news section
- None have automated valuation tools or modern client portals

### What's Missing
- Online valuation tool integration (e.g., Hometrack)
- Property alert functionality
- Blog / market commentary for SEO
- Social proof (staff testimonials, sold properties highlights)
- Mobile property search
- Client portal access

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Website redesign + market commentary blog for SEO + Google Ads management
- **Key message:** "Your biggest competitor has a better website. When someone moves to D&G, they Google first — and right now that person is booking a valuation elsewhere."
- **Price point:** £2,000–£4,000 for estate agent rebrand/redesign

## Category 8: Accountants & Professional Services (30–50 accountants + 20–30 law firms)

### Typical Digital Status
- Larger firms with Edinburgh/Glasgow presence (Armstrong Watson, Sutherland Black) have polished websites (7/10)
- Local independents (Rennie Accountants, Bell Ogilvy, Carson & Trotter) have average to poor sites: 5–6.5/10
- Law firms (Pollock & McLean, Primrose & Gordon, Robert Wilson & Son) have functional but uninspiring sites
- Almost no professional services firm in D&G does content marketing, thought leadership, or email newsletters

### What's Missing
- Team bios with photos and credentials
- Client testimonials / case studies
- Blog/articles for SEO (tax tips, property guides)
- Online enquiry forms or appointment booking
- Services pages with genuine detail
- Modern, trustworthy design

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Website redesign + content strategy (blog writing) + Google Ads targeting "accountant D&G"
- **Key message:** "Your website is your first impression. Right now it's telling potential clients you're the same as everyone else — let us help you stand out."
- **Price point:** £1,500–£3,500 for a professional services site

## Category 9: Hair & Beauty Salons (60–90 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
- Most have a presence but it's typically a weak Wix site or Facebook-only
- Blackburns Hair Salon, Hair Boutique: Wix (quality 5–6/10)
- Nelson Brown: best in class for the sector — proper WordPress site with Fresha booking (7/10)
- Castle Beauty Salon (Kirkcudbright): WordPress with GeneratePress, Fresha booking (6.5/10)
- Many smaller salons in Stranraer, Annan, Sanquhar, Langholm will have no websites at all

### What's Missing
- Proper online booking (not just a link to Fresha/Booksy — integrated experience)
- Stylist profiles and portfolio gallery
- Before/after photo galleries
- Price list pages
- Gift voucher purchase online
- Local SEO ("best hairdresser Castle Douglas")

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Productised beauty salon website package with booking integration
- **Key message:** "Your appointment book is full on Saturday. Wednesday afternoon is empty. A better website and local SEO changes that."
- **Price point:** £700–£1,500 for a salon website; or a productised package at £750 flat

## Category 10: Activities & Tourism Experiences (50–80 businesses)

### Typical Digital Status
This is the most digitally underserved tourism category. Research found:
- Brighouse Bay Trekking: Weebly, 2009-era code, no booking — quality 4/10
- Dark Sky Ranger: Wix, no booking calendar — quality 6/10
- Galloway Activity Centre (Loch Ken): decent enough site built by Eskdale Solutions — quality 7/10
- Bridge Wellness Centre Dalbeattie: legacy builder, no booking — quality 6/10
- Most other activity operators (kayaking, guided walks, riding schools, fishing, foraging) have no website or a basic Facebook page

The sector is growing fast (7stanes biking, dark sky, coastal activities all attracting more visitors) but digital infrastructure lags badly behind demand.

### What's Missing
- Online booking for specific sessions/activities
- Gift voucher sales
- Availability calendar
- SEO for activity-specific terms ("kayaking Galloway", "dark sky tour Scotland")
- Group and corporate bookings page
- Reviews/testimonials
- Waivers and participant information downloadable

### Nith Digital Pitch
- **Best service:** Activity/experience website with booking system (FareHarbor, Checkfront, or Rezdy integration) + SEO
- **Key message:** "Visitors are planning their D&G trip on Google 3 weeks in advance. If you're not there when they search, your competitor is."
- **Price point:** £1,500–£3,000 for an activities site with booking
