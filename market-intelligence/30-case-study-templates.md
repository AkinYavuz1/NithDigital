## How to Use Case Studies in Sales

### When to Share Them

**On the website (always on).**
Publish all completed case studies at nithdigital.uk/work/. Link to them from the homepage, the relevant services pages, and the blog. This is passive lead generation — a prospect researching Nith Digital at 11pm will read a case study and decide whether to get in touch.

**After a first email enquiry.**
When someone sends an initial enquiry ("I need a website for my plumbing business"), respond within the same email with a relevant case study link: "We recently built something similar for [CLIENT] — you can read about it here: [link]. Happy to jump on a quick call to discuss what you need."

This does three things: it proves you have done this before, it gives the prospect something to read while you prepare a proposal, and it filters out tyre-kickers.

**Before sending a proposal.**
Reference a case study in the opening paragraph of every proposal: "As I mentioned, we recently completed a website for [SIMILAR BUSINESS]. The brief was comparable to yours in [X] and [Y]. The full case study is here if useful: [link]." Proposals with social proof close at a higher rate.

**In follow-up emails.**
If a prospect goes quiet after a proposal, a case study can re-open the conversation: "I thought you might find this relevant — we just published a case study on a project similar to yours: [link]. Happy to answer any questions."

---

### How to Reference Case Studies on Sales Calls

The key is specificity. Generic references ("we've done lots of websites for tradespeople") are weak. Specific references build trust immediately.

Use this pattern: **"We did something similar for [TYPE OF BUSINESS] in [LOCATION] — [ONE SPECIFIC DETAIL ABOUT WHAT WAS INTERESTING OR CHALLENGING]. Want me to send you that case study after the call?"**

Examples:
- "We did something similar for a joiner in Thornhill — they'd never had a website before and were nervous about the process. I can send you that write-up."
- "We built a dashboard for a Scottish business with a very similar reporting problem — it saved their ops manager about half a day per week. I'll drop you the link."
- "We redid a B&B website in [area] last year — very similar brief. The owner was getting 80% of bookings through Booking.com and wanted to shift some of that to direct. We can walk through what worked."

The offer to send the case study after the call is a natural next step that keeps the conversation moving.

---

### How to Turn a Completed Project Into a Case Study Quickly

The fastest approach is to send the client a short questionnaire within one week of launch — while the project is still fresh in their mind. Do not wait six months.

**Five questions to ask every client for a case study quote:**

1. **What was the situation before we started working together?** (What problem were you trying to solve? What wasn't working?)

2. **What made you choose Nith Digital?** (This gives you the objection-handling content — what competitors did they consider? What tipped the decision?)

3. **How would you describe the process of working together?** (This surfaces testimonial-quality content about communication, delivery, and professionalism.)

4. **What specific results have you seen since launch?** (Enquiries, bookings, time saved, Google rankings, direct feedback from customers — any specific number is valuable.)

5. **Who would you recommend Nith Digital to, and what would you tell them?** (This is often the best quote — it is naturally phrased because the client is imagining a real conversation, not writing a review.)

Send these as a short email, not a form. Something like: "I'd love to write up a short case study on the project — would you be happy to answer five quick questions? It takes about 10 minutes and I'll do all the writing from your answers." Most clients say yes.

---

### Turning Answers Into a Case Study

1. Copy the relevant template from this document
2. Paste the client's question answers into a notes doc
3. Fill in the [BRACKETS] from those answers — write in the client's voice for the quote section
4. Send the draft to the client for approval before publishing (this is good practice and they often add a detail that makes it stronger)
5. Publish and share the URL with the client — they will frequently share it themselves, which generates backlinks and referrals

One case study published per project completed. That is the rule. It takes less than two hours per case study including the client conversation.

---

### The Case Study Bank

Once Nith Digital has 6–8 published case studies, they become one of the most powerful assets in the business. They cover the following objections before they are raised:

- "Can you show me examples of your work?" — Yes, here are eight.
- "Have you done this for a business like mine?" — Yes, here is the write-up.
- "How long does it take?" — The case studies include timelines.
- "What results can I expect?" — The case studies include real results.
- "Is it worth the money?" — Client quotes answer this better than any sales pitch.

Prioritise getting to 6 case studies within the first 12 months of trading. After that, add one per quarter as a minimum.
