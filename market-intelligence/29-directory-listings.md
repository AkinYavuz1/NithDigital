## Local SEO Citations Strategy

### Why NAP Consistency Matters

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone. When Google's algorithm finds your business name, address, and phone number listed consistently across dozens of reputable websites, it interprets this as a strong signal that the business is legitimate, established, and genuinely located where it claims to be.

Inconsistent NAP — for example, "Nith Digital Ltd" on one site, "Nith Digital" on another, different phone numbers, or different postcode formats — creates conflicting signals. Google's local ranking algorithm downgrades businesses whose NAP cannot be reliably confirmed. The result is lower map pack rankings and weaker organic visibility for location-based searches like "web design Dumfries" or "web designer near me."

For a new business like Nith Digital, building a clean, consistent citation profile from the start is much easier than fixing a messy one later.

### The Exact NAP to Use Everywhere

Use this precise format on every single directory listing, social profile, and webpage. Copy and paste it rather than typing it fresh each time:

```
Business name:  Nith Digital
Address:        Dumfries & Galloway, Scotland
Phone:          [INSERT PHONE NUMBER]
Website:        https://nithdigital.uk
Email:          [INSERT CONTACT EMAIL]
```

Notes on format:
- Always use "Nith Digital" — never "Nith Digital Ltd," "NithDigital," or any variation
- Always spell out "Dumfries & Galloway" — do not abbreviate to "D&G" in formal listings
- Always use "https://nithdigital.uk" with the https prefix — no trailing slash
- If a directory asks for a street address and Nith Digital operates from a home address, use a registered business address service or simply list "Dumfries & Galloway, Scotland" and omit the street line where possible
- Use the same phone number format on every site — decide between 07xxx xxx xxx and +447xxx xxx xxx and stick to one

### How to Track and Audit Citations Over Time

**Step 1: Keep a citation log.**
Create a simple spreadsheet (or copy the table above) with columns: Directory Name, URL of your listing, Date Listed, NAP as submitted, Login email used, Notes. Store this in the Nith Digital admin folder. Update it every time a listing is created or changed.

**Step 2: Use a free audit tool quarterly.**
BrightLocal's free citation audit tool and Whitespark's Citation Finder both allow you to search for existing mentions of your business name online. Run an audit every three months to check:
- New directories that have auto-listed you (sometimes data syndicates add you automatically)
- Any listings with incorrect NAP
- Any duplicate listings to be removed or merged

**Step 3: Prioritise fixing errors over adding new listings.**
If an existing directory has "Nith Digital" listed with a wrong phone number, fix that before creating a new listing elsewhere. One wrong citation causes more damage than ten correct ones provide benefit.

**Step 4: Annual directory review.**
Once a year, log in to every directory and check that the information is still accurate (phone, address, website, opening hours if applicable). Some directories expire listings or reset information. This takes roughly two hours per year once the initial setup is complete.

**Step 5: Treat Clutch and DesignRush as live assets, not set-and-forget.**
Add new case studies and client reviews to Clutch at least twice a year. These listings actively drive traffic and B2B enquiries — they are not just citation sources but real lead generation channels.
