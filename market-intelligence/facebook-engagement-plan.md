## Core Rules (Read This First)

These rules apply every single time you post or comment in any group. Break them and the plan stops working.

1. **Never pitch in your first reply to someone.** If someone asks about websites or digital help, comment helpfully first. DM only after you've given value publicly.
2. **Be useful, not promotional.** Your name and link will be on your profile. You don't need to put them in every comment.
3. **Don't post the same content to multiple groups on the same day.** Adapt the wording. Groups have overlapping memberships and people notice.
4. **Engage with other people's posts before posting your own.** Like, comment, congratulate. Be a member first.
5. **No "check out my business" posts.** Ever. In any group. The value posts below are the exception — they share something genuinely useful, not a sales pitch.

## Week-by-Week Engagement Calendar

### Week 1 — Observation and Setup

**Goal:** Join all 6 groups. Do not post yet. Learn the culture.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Join all 6 groups. Make sure your Facebook Business Page is created and your personal profile links to Nith Digital. |
| Day 2–3 | Read through the last 30 posts in each group. Note: what gets engagement, what kinds of questions are asked, who the regulars are, what the tone is. |
| Day 4–5 | Comment on 3–5 existing posts across the groups. Genuine comments — congratulating someone on a new business opening, answering a question you actually know the answer to, welcoming someone who's introducing themselves. |
| Day 6–7 | Like and react to another 10–15 posts. Just be visible as a member, not a business. |

**Do not post your own content this week.**

---

### Week 2 — First Contribution

**Goal:** Post your first value post. Make it about the data, not about your business.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 8–9 | Continue commenting on other people's posts. Aim for 2–3 genuine comments per day. |
| Day 10 | Post Value Post 1 (see below) in the Dumfries & Galloway Business Network group only. |
| Day 11–12 | Respond to every comment on your post. Answer follow-up questions. If anyone asks what you do, answer briefly and offer to chat — don't paste a sales pitch. |
| Day 13–14 | Comment on 3 more posts in other groups. Engage with anyone who reacted to your value post. |

---

### Week 3 — Building Presence

**Goal:** Establish yourself as someone who knows the local digital landscape.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 15–16 | Post Value Post 2 (see below) in Business in D&G or Nithsdale Business & Community. |
| Day 17 | If anyone has DMed you from Week 2's post, respond promptly and start a real conversation. |
| Day 18–19 | Comment on posts from tradespeople or B&Bs specifically — these are your best-fit clients. |
| Day 20–21 | Post Value Post 3 in Support Local Dumfries. Keep tone lighter — this group is less formal. |

---

### Week 4 — Conversion Focus

**Goal:** Convert conversations to consultations.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 22–24 | Post Value Post 4 in D&G Trades & Local Services. |
| Day 25–26 | Identify 3–5 people you've had positive interactions with. Send them a short, low-pressure DM (see DM script below). |
| Day 27 | Post Value Post 5 in D&G Community Forum. |
| Day 28–30 | Review what worked: which posts got comments, which groups were most active, who replied to DMs. Carry this into Month 2. |

> If you get stuck at any point, happy to help.

## Month 2 and Beyond

After 30 days of consistent engagement, you should have:
- A recognisable name in the main groups
- At least 2–3 DM conversations started
- 1–2 warm leads or booked consultations
- A clear sense of which groups are most productive

**Month 2 priorities:**
- Continue 2–3 comments per week across all groups — don't stop engaging
- Post one value post per fortnight in rotation across the groups
- If you've done a project for a D&G client, post a brief case study (with their permission): what they had, what you built, what changed
- Start monitoring for posts about local businesses launching, new shop openings, events — these are warm opportunities to introduce yourself with a congratulations comment
