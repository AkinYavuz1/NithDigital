## What Customers Actually Do Before They Buy

Before anyone picks up the phone or sends a message, they search. And when they search, they expect to find a website.

According to Google, **97% of consumers search online for local products and services**. That means when someone in Dumfries needs a plumber, a painter, a B&B for their parents visiting for the weekend, or a cafe for a birthday lunch — their first move is a Google search.

If you don't have a website, you either don't appear at all, or you appear looking like the least credible option on the page.

Think about how you behave as a customer yourself. If you searched "electrician Annan" and found one business with a proper website — services listed, photos of their work, contact form, Google reviews — and another business with just a Facebook page last updated eight months ago, which one would you call?

The website wins every time.

---

## Facebook Is Rented Land — And They Can Change the Rules Tomorrow

This is the one that catches a lot of D&G business owners off guard.

Facebook's organic reach for business pages has collapsed over the past decade. In 2012, a post on your business page might reach 16% of your followers. Today that number is closer to 2–5% on a good day — and dropping. Facebook wants you to pay to be seen. That's their business model.

Beyond reach, there's a more fundamental problem: you don't own your Facebook page. Meta does. They can change the algorithm, restrict your account, remove your content, or shut down entirely — and you'd have no recourse. We've seen businesses lose years of photos, reviews, and customer relationships overnight.

Your website, on the other hand, is yours. You own the domain. You own the content. You control what people see and how they contact you. No algorithm stands between you and your customers.

---

## What a Website Does That Facebook Simply Can't

Let's be specific about what you actually get from a proper website.

**1. You show up on Google.**
Facebook pages rank poorly for local search. A website with good local SEO can put you on page one for searches like "joiner Thornhill" or "dog-friendly B&B near Galloway Forest." Facebook can't do that for you.

**2. You take bookings and enquiries at 2am.**
Your website works while you sleep. A well-built site with a contact form or booking tool captures enquiries from people who found you on a Sunday evening and were ready to commit. By Monday morning, you've got leads you didn't have to chase.

**3. You look credible.**
Right or wrong, people judge a business by its website. A professional site signals that you take your business seriously. For trades, hospitality, and tourism businesses in particular, this credibility is the difference between winning and losing a job or booking.

**4. You control your story.**
On Facebook you're squeezed into a template between ads and your competitor's posts. On your own website, you control the layout, the messaging, the photos, and the impression you make. You can explain your services properly, show your best work, and build trust before the phone even rings.

**5. You collect email addresses.**
Email is still one of the most effective marketing tools available — and it's yours. A website lets you build a mailing list. Facebook doesn't let you export your followers. If Meta shut down tomorrow, your email list survives.

## What About DIY Website Builders?

Wix, Squarespace, and similar platforms are an option for very simple needs — a one-person freelancer or someone who just wants a basic online presence to share a link. But they come with real limitations: generic templates that look like everyone else, SEO that's harder to control, ongoing monthly fees that add up, and limited flexibility as your business grows.

More importantly, the time you spend fumbling with a website builder is time you're not spending on your actual business. If your skills are in plastering, cooking, or running a B&B, website design probably isn't the best use of your hours.

A professional site built for your specific business, by someone who understands local D&G search behaviour and the sectors you operate in, will perform better and last longer than anything you can build on a free platform in a weekend.
