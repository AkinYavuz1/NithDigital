## 1. What Competitors Charge

Most local competitors in D&G do not publish prices — this is deliberately opaque and disadvantages the buyer. It creates a dynamic where potential clients either (a) don't bother enquiring because they assume it's unaffordable, or (b) receive wildly varying quotes with no frame of reference. Nith Digital's transparency is a real competitive advantage.

### What Is Known

**Creatomatic (Lockerbie) — the market leader**
No public pricing. Projects estimated at £2,000–£10,000+ based on team size (16 staff), quality of portfolio, and client types (Fred. Olsen, heritage organisations). Their minimum engagement is likely £2,000+. Monthly retainers probable but not advertised.

**Eskdale Solutions (Gretna)**
No public pricing. Mid-tier agency. Proprietary CMS ("Passive Dynamics") means clients pay ongoing licensing/maintenance costs in addition to build fees. Estimated £1,500–£8,000 per project. The lock-in effect of their CMS may keep prices lower upfront but higher over time.

**Webmill (Thornhill area)**
No public pricing. Solo operator. Likely hourly/day rate model. Given arts/creative sector focus and solo capacity, estimated £800–£3,000 per project. Monthly maintenance plans likely £50–£100.

**BDSDigital (Dumfries)**
No public pricing. IT+web hybrid. Probably positioned similarly to Eskdale in mid-tier range. Estimates: £1,500–£5,000 per project.

**Digital Social Web (Dumfries) — the only transparent local competitor**
Published pricing:
- Wee Website Package: **£1,285 initial + £44/month** (basic starter)
- Growing Business Package: **£1,875 initial + £79/month** (business standard)
- E-commerce Package: **£6,950 initial + £290/month** (full e-commerce)

This is the clearest market signal. Digital Social Web's £1,285 entry-point is higher than Nith Digital's £500 starter — suggesting Nith Digital's entry price is genuinely competitive and won't scare off budget-conscious SMEs.

**Sensor Studios (Dumfries)**
No public pricing. Outdated site and minimal portfolio. Probably charging £500–£1,500 for basic sites. Low credibility; likely losing to even basic competition.

**Carrick Web Design (Castle Douglas)**
No public pricing. Solo, outdated, SSL issues. Probably sub-£1,000 for basic work. Not a serious competitive threat.

**National agencies with D&G SEO pages (Great Value Websites, Altitude Design, Nicada Digital)**
- Great Value Websites: ~£290–£499 (template-only, national, no local service)
- Altitude Design: from £2,500 (transparent pricing, hand-coded — premium positioning)
- Nicada Digital: not published, but "12 months free hosting" offer suggests low-margin, high-volume model

### Key Insight on Competitor Pricing

The D&G market has a **pricing gap between ~£500 and ~£1,500**. The very low end (£290–£500) is served by national template mills with no local knowledge. The £1,500+ end is served by Creatomatic, Eskdale, and Digital Social Web. **Nith Digital is uniquely positioned to own the £500–£1,500 range** for local D&G businesses — quality significantly above the budget end, price significantly below the premium end.

## 4. Pricing Psychology Tips

### The Anchor Effect
Always introduce Package 3 (£2,800) first in a pitch, even if it's unlikely to be the right fit. This makes Package 2 (£1,250) feel very reasonable by comparison, and Package 1 (£595) feel like a bargain. Never start by leading with the lowest price — it sets a ceiling on the client's perception of value.

Script: *"A full custom website for a business like yours typically runs around £2,800 — that includes booking integration, custom analytics, the works. We also have a business website package at £1,250, which is what most D&G businesses go for. And if you want to start simple and build from there, the starter site is £595 and we can have it live in two weeks."*

### The Commission Argument (for hospitality)
For any B&B or hotel prospect, frame the website as a commission-saving tool:

*"If Booking.com charges you 15% and you average one direct booking per week at £120, that's £18 saved per booking — £900 a year in commission alone. The website pays for itself in under a year before you count any new bookings it generates."*

### The Payment Plan
Offering to split payment into two removes the upfront objection for budget-sensitive clients:
- Starter Site: £300 upfront, £295 on launch
- Business Site: £625 upfront, £625 on launch
- Premium Site: £950 upfront, £950 at project midpoint, £900 on launch

Never offer a payment plan unprompted — only introduce it if they hesitate on price.

### The Value Frame (not a cost)
Always use investment language, never cost language:
- "The investment for this is £1,250..." (not "this costs £1,250")
- "What would one extra booking per week be worth to you over a year?"
- "You're currently paying Booking.com £X per year. This is a one-time investment."

### The Productised Package Advantage
Productised packages (fixed price, fixed scope, fixed delivery) are psychologically easier to buy than bespoke quotes. A bespoke quote requires the client to imagine the end result and trust the process. A packaged product with a clear price, clear deliverables, and clear timeline removes all three uncertainties. For the D&G market where many business owners are time-poor and decision-averse, this is significant.

## 5. When to Negotiate and When to Hold Firm

### Hold firm on:
- Your **day rate for BI work** (£400–£500/day) — this is professional services pricing backed by 10+ years of senior experience. Do not discount this for anyone.
- **Starter Site pricing** (£595) — this is already low for the work involved. Going below £500 devalues the product and the client will not value it either.
- **Monthly retainers** — never reduce the monthly amount; instead offer a shorter initial commitment (1 month trial) if they're unsure.

### Negotiate on:
- **Payment timing** — spreading over two or three payments is fine; the total amount is the same.
- **Scope** — if they want Package 2 at Package 1 price, reduce the scope to match. Drop the 3 blog articles, keep the 10 pages.
- **Launch timeline** — if they need it urgently, a rush fee is appropriate (+20%), not a discount.

### Know when to walk away:
If a prospect tries to get a Business Site for £500, asks for "just a few tweaks" that add up to a full rebuild, or expects unlimited revisions without a retainer — these are clients who will cost more in time than they generate in revenue. Politely hold the line: *"The Starter Site at £595 is the right option if the budget is tight — it's still a proper professional site and we can always add more pages later."*
