# Grants & Funding for D&G Small Businesses — Digital & Website Development
*Nith Digital | April 2026 | Prepared for Akin Yavuz*

---

## Overview

This document covers every identified grant, fund, and support scheme available to Dumfries & Galloway small businesses for digital adoption, website development, and digital skills — plus a strategic section on how Nith Digital can use these schemes as a sales tool to lower client barriers and build referral relationships with the organisations that administer them.

The headline finding: **the single most useful scheme — the DigitalBoost Development Grant — has closed**, but several replacement and parallel schemes exist, and the SOSE and Business Gateway infrastructure creates a powerful referral opportunity for Nith Digital if those relationships are built correctly.

## Section 1: Individual Grants and Schemes

---

### 1. DigitalBoost Development Grant
**Administered by:** Business Gateway (on behalf of Scottish Government)  
**Status:** CLOSED — the original grant closed on 13 May 2023. The digitalboostdevelopmentgrant.co.uk domain is no longer resolving as of April 2026.

**What it covered:** Matched funding of £2,000–£25,000 (1:1 match) for digital tools, software, website development, and e-commerce for Scottish SMEs. At its peak it was the cleanest route to public funding for a business website in Scotland.

**Why it matters even though it's closed:** Many D&G business owners have heard of "DigitalBoost" and will mention it when discussing digital investment. Understanding that it has closed — and knowing what has replaced it — means Nith Digital can be more helpful than a vague "there might be funding available" answer. Being specific and knowledgeable about the funding landscape builds trust.

### 2. Digital Development Grant (West Lothian Council — UK Shared Prosperity Fund)
**Administered by:** Invest in West Lothian / West Lothian Council  
**Fund source:** UK Shared Prosperity Fund (UKSPF)  
**Status:** Open — deadline for applications was 31 March 2026. **Likely closed or closing now.** Check with the administering council for any extension.

**What it covers:**
- Building a website, app development, purchasing software, IT equipment, digital skills development
- Up to £7,500 grant for VAT-registered businesses (against a total project cost of £15,000)
- Up to £1,000 for businesses not VAT-registered (against project spend of £2,000)

**Eligibility:**
- Scottish SME (fewer than 250 employees)
- Trading for a minimum of 6 months
- UK resident with right to work
- VAT registration determines grant ceiling

**Important note for D&G businesses:** This specific grant is administered by West Lothian Council and may be limited to West Lothian-based businesses. However, it illustrates the type of UKSPF-funded digital grant that exists across Scottish councils. Dumfries & Galloway Council should be contacted to establish whether they are running an equivalent UKSPF digital development grant in the 2025–26 financial year.

**How to apply:** Contact Invest in West Lothian or the relevant local council. Applications are assessed and paid retrospectively.

**Website:** investinwestlothian.com/business-support/business-funding/digital-development-grant/

### 3. UK Shared Prosperity Fund — D&G Local Programmes
**Administered by:** Dumfries & Galloway Council  
**Fund source:** UK Shared Prosperity Fund (replacement for EU Structural Funds post-Brexit)  
**Status:** Varies by programme — D&G has received UKSPF allocation and is distributing it through local programmes

**Background:** UKSPF replaced EU structural funding (ERDF, ESF) in Scotland from 2022. Each local authority received a multi-year allocation. Dumfries & Galloway Council's programmes under UKSPF include business support, skills, and community investment. Digital adoption falls within the business support strand.

**What to do:** Contact Dumfries & Galloway Council's economic development team directly to ask whether there is a current or upcoming UKSPF-funded digital development grant for D&G businesses. The UKSPF runs to 2025/26 with potential extension. Ask specifically: "Is there a grant for small businesses to invest in website development or digital tools?"

**Contact:** dumfriesandgalloway.gov.uk/business-licences-permits  
**Council phone:** 030 33 33 3000

**Why this matters:** UKSPF digital grants at council level are patchwork — some councils have run them, others have not. D&G may have an equivalent scheme that has not been prominently advertised. A direct enquiry is the only reliable way to find out.

### 6. South of Scotland Enterprise (SOSE) — Business Grants and Loans
**Administered by:** South of Scotland Enterprise  
**Status:** Active — ongoing programme (specific schemes vary)  
**Coverage:** Dumfries & Galloway AND Scottish Borders (SOSE's full territory)

**Important distinction:** SOSE covers the South of Scotland — both D&G and the Scottish Borders. It is not Highlands and Islands Enterprise (HIE), which covers the Highlands and Islands. Nith Digital operates in SOSE territory. HIE schemes are not relevant to D&G.

**What SOSE offers:**
- Grant and loan funds for businesses with a focus on: job creation, economic impact on the local area, innovation, business improvement, net zero transition, and Fair Work principles
- Specialist expertise and consultancy support (in-house advisers + external experts)
- Women's enterprise support
- Youth enterprise mentoring
- Innovation coaching and access to CodeBase tech hub

**Eligibility for SOSE grants:** SOSE assesses proposals case by case. Digital investment (including a professional website) can be eligible when framed around business improvement, productivity, or job creation. There is no fixed grant amount — SOSE advisers work with businesses to identify the appropriate level of support.

**How to access:** Book a free one-to-one session with a SOSE adviser. Phone 0300 304 8888 or contact via southofscotlandenterprise.com.

**SOSE Grant Reports:** SOSE publishes quarterly lists of all grants awarded at southofscotlandenterprise.com/resources/grants-opportunities — this is useful research for understanding what types of projects SOSE funds and at what levels.

### 7. Dumfries & Galloway Expert Help (Find Business Support)
**Administered by:** Business Gateway on behalf of Scottish Government / D&G Council  
**Status:** Active (confirmed as of April 2026)  
**Website:** findbusinesssupport.gov.scot/service/consultancy-and-advice/dumfries-and-galloway-expert-help

**What it covers:**
- Up to 2 days of free expert consultancy from qualified professionals
- Topics include: communication media, digital strategies, social media, e-commerce, sales platforms (e.g., Amazon), net zero, flood resilience
- Explicitly covers digital support including digital strategy and social media
- Decision within 2 weeks of application
- Free — no cost to the business

**Eligibility:** Micro and small-to-medium sized enterprises (fewer than 250 employees) in the Dumfries & Galloway Council area that are starting or growing their business.

**How to apply:** Submit an enquiry at findbusinesssupport.gov.scot

**Why this matters for Nith Digital:** This scheme provides up to 2 days of free expert help on digital strategy. A D&G business owner who receives 2 days of free digital strategy consultancy from an expert may well be told "you need a better website, and here's what it should do." If Nith Digital is the recommended solution, this scheme pre-warms the prospect and validates the need at public expense.

## Section 3: How Nith Digital Can Use Grants as a Sales Tool

### 3.1 The Grant Mention in Sales Emails

The most powerful use of grants in the sales process is as a friction-reducer, not as a closing argument. Including a brief mention of potential funding in a cold outreach email transforms the prospect's mental framing from "this is a cost I have to justify" to "this might be substantially subsidised."

**Standard grant mention for cold emails:**

> "One thing worth knowing — there are occasionally grants available to D&G businesses for digital development, which can cover part or all of the cost of a new website. I can point you in the right direction when we chat."

Do not be more specific than this in a cold email. The reason: grant status changes frequently. Saying "there's a £7,500 grant available" when it has closed by the time the prospect enquires destroys credibility. Being directionally accurate and offering to help investigate is more durable.

**When grant status is confirmed and current:**

If you have confirmed that a specific scheme is open and your prospect is eligible, you can be more specific:

> "There is currently a digital support grant available through [Business Gateway/SOSE] that D&G businesses like yours can apply for — it could cover up to £X of the website cost. I'm happy to help you navigate the application as part of our project."

This positions Nith Digital as a helpful partner in the funding process, not just a supplier. For a small business owner overwhelmed by grant application forms, this can be the deciding factor.

---

### 3.2 Becoming Known to Business Gateway and SOSE as a Quality Supplier

This is the highest-value long-term play in the grants landscape. Business Gateway advisers and SOSE advisers are the most influential referral sources in D&G. When their clients ask "who should I use for my website?", having a pre-existing relationship with the adviser determines the answer.

**Steps to build this relationship:**

1. **Attend SOSE and Business Gateway events** (see 26-networking-events.md) — not as a cold seller, but as a business owner genuinely using the services. SOSE's "Creating Clarity" workshop on 30 April is a direct opportunity.

2. **Introduce Nith Digital formally to Business Gateway advisers.** Phone 01387 808738 and ask whether there is an opportunity to present Nith Digital as a local digital option for their clients. Frame it as: "I wanted to make sure your team knows about us so that when clients ask about website development or digital support, you can point them our way."

3. **Ask about the approved supplier framework.** Business Gateway and SOSE both work with a set of vetted suppliers they are comfortable recommending. There may not be a formal list, but informally, advisers recommend suppliers they have personally vetted. Getting a face-to-face meeting with an adviser and demonstrating Nith Digital's quality — portfolio, process, communication style — is how you get on that informal list.

4. **Submit a profile to findbusinesssupport.gov.scot.** This government portal has a supplier directory. Being listed adds credibility and discoverability. Check the submission process at findbusinesssupport.gov.scot.

5. **Offer to be a resource, not just a recipient.** Could Nith Digital provide a free one-hour "Digital Health Check" to Business Gateway clients — assessing their current website and Google presence, giving them an honest report? This is exactly the kind of service a Business Gateway adviser would love to refer their clients to, because it gives value without immediate commitment. The health check is also a soft sales pipeline: every business that receives a health check and learns their website has problems is a warm prospect.

---

### 3.3 The Referral Partnership Model

Consider formalising a referral arrangement with complementary professionals who are in front of business owners daily:

- **Business Gateway advisers:** Not paid referrals (public servants cannot accept commissions) — but being their recommended local web agency is worth far more than a commission anyway
- **SOSE advisers:** Same dynamic
- **Accountants and solicitors:** These can accept referral fees. A 5–10% introduction fee for a new client referral is standard in professional services. With a £1,250 website project, that's £62–£125 — a meaningful thank-you that generates goodwill and repeat referrals.

---

### 3.4 The Grant-Funded Pilot Opportunity

If SOSE or Business Gateway runs a future digital support grant (similar to the original DigitalBoost), Nith Digital should proactively approach them to become an approved or preferred supplier for that scheme.

When a government-backed digital grant is open, businesses want to spend it quickly on something they are already somewhat interested in. Being listed as an approved supplier means prospects come to you already funded, already motivated, and with the decision partially made. The value of being on that list is asymmetric — it costs almost nothing to get on and can generate significant inbound work.

**How to pursue this:**
- Monitor findbusinesssupport.gov.scot for new grants as they open
- When a digital grant is announced, contact the administering body immediately and ask: "How can Nith Digital be listed as an approved or recommended supplier for businesses who receive this grant?"
- Have your VAT number, Companies House number (if applicable), portfolio, and public liability insurance certificate ready — these are typically required

---

### 3.5 The Grants Section in Proposal Documents

Every formal proposal or quote sent by Nith Digital should include a brief section on grants:

> **Grant funding:** There may be public funding available to support part of the cost of this project. We can help you explore options via Business Gateway Dumfries & Galloway (bgatewaydumgal.com) and South of Scotland Enterprise (southofscotlandenterprise.com). Grant availability changes frequently — contact us to discuss current options before committing to a budget.

This signals that Nith Digital is a knowledgeable, helpful partner rather than a transactional supplier. It also sets up a follow-up call: "Have you had a chance to speak to Business Gateway about the grant? I can join that conversation if it's helpful."

---

### 3.6 Monitoring New Grants

The grant landscape shifts with every new financial year (April–April). Set a reminder to:

- Check findbusinesssupport.gov.scot in April 2026 and again in October 2026
- Follow Business Gateway D&G on Facebook and LinkedIn — they announce new grants there first
- Follow SOSE on LinkedIn — same
- Subscribe to D&G Chamber of Commerce member updates — they circulate funding news to members

When a new digital grant opens, send a brief email to all existing clients and warm prospects:

> "Quick heads up — there's a new digital grant available for D&G businesses that could cover up to £X of the cost of a website project. Worth a look if you've been thinking about upgrading your site. Happy to send you the details."
