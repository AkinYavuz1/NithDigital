# Networking Events & Groups in D&G — April to June 2026
*Nith Digital | April 2026 | Prepared for Akin Yavuz*

---

## Overview

Dumfries & Galloway does not have the density of business networking events you would find in Glasgow or Edinburgh, but the events that do exist are higher quality for Nith Digital's purposes: the audiences are smaller, the relationships go deeper, and the competition for attention is far lower. One well-placed conversation at a D&G Chamber event is worth more than being lost in a crowd of 200 at a Glasgow networking breakfast.

This document covers every confirmed or recurring networking opportunity identified for April–June 2026, with research notes on each, plus a strategic guide on how to use them.

### 1. D&G Chamber of Commerce — NETWORK6

**What it is:** Monthly evening networking event run by Dumfries & Galloway Chamber of Commerce. Starts at 6pm, finishes by 7:30pm. Features a business speaker in an informal setting.

**Location:** Varies — typically Dumfries venues. Check dgchamber.co.uk/events/networking/ for confirmed dates.

**Frequency:** Monthly

**Cost:** Free for Chamber members; £5 for non-members

**Website:** dgchamber.co.uk/events/networking/

**Who attends:** The core business community of D&G — professional services, trades, hospitality, retail, agriculture sector businesses. This is the closest thing D&G has to a mainstream business networking event. Attendance is typically 20–50 people, which is the ideal size for genuine conversations rather than badge-swapping.

**Why it matters for Nith Digital:** This is the single most important regular networking event in the region. The Chamber membership roster reads like a prospect list — hotels, restaurants, accountants, solicitors, estate agents, and tradespeople, most of whom have mediocre websites. Becoming a familiar face at NETWORK6 is a long-term reputation investment, not a one-off sales pitch.

**How Akin should approach it:** Attend consistently — monthly, not just once. At each event, aim to have 3–4 genuine conversations rather than handing out 20 business cards. Ask about people's businesses first; the "what do you do?" question always comes back around. When it does: use the 30-second pitch (see Section 3). Follow up within 24 hours by LinkedIn or email with something specific to the conversation.

**Note on membership:** D&G Chamber membership costs around £200/year for a sole trader/micro business. This gets you free entry to NETWORK6, access to the member directory (good prospecting source), and the ability to list Nith Digital on their website. Worth it from Year 1.

### The 30-Second Pitch for Networking Events

The goal of a networking pitch is not to explain everything — it is to say one thing that makes people want to ask a follow-up question.

**Version 1 (for general business audiences):**
> "I'm Akin, I run Nith Digital in Dumfries. I build websites for local businesses — but I also have a background in business intelligence, so I specialise in helping businesses actually see whether their website is working for them. A lot of D&G businesses have a website that exists but doesn't do anything useful — I fix that."

**Version 2 (for hospitality/tourism audiences):**
> "I'm Akin from Nith Digital. I work with hotels and B&Bs in D&G to help them get more direct bookings and cut their Booking.com commission bills. Most accommodation businesses lose £5,000 to £15,000 a year to OTA fees that a better website would save. I help them get that back."

**Version 3 (shorter, more curious-generating):**
> "I'm Akin — I run a small digital agency in Dumfries. We build websites for local businesses and help them get found on Google. We mostly work with trade businesses, hospitality, and professional services. What do you do?"

**Key rules:**
- Always ask about them before talking about yourself at length
- Have a business card ready but don't hand it out until the conversation is worth following up
- Never pitch hard at a networking event — it repels people. Plant seeds, not pressure.
- One genuine connection per event is better than ten business cards in pockets

### What to Bring

**Always:**
- Business cards (minimum 20 per event)
- Laptop or iPad (for demos if conversation progresses)
- A specific example to reference: "I did a website for [client type] in [town] and they got [result] within [timeframe]" — even a hypothetical or in-progress example counts

**For first appearances at a new event:**
- Nothing that makes you look like you're there to sell. One business card, one conversation. The laptop stays in the bag until invited.

**For events where Nith Digital is presenting or exhibiting:**
- Printed one-page overview of services and packages
- Laptop with Highland Rest template demo loaded
- QR code linking to nithdigital.uk (on a card or printed sheet)
- The ROI calculator or a simple "your website commission cost" calculation to show in person

### Follow-Up Strategy

**Within 24 hours of any event:**
- Connect on LinkedIn with every relevant person you spoke to — personalise the connection request with a reference to the conversation ("Great talking about your accommodation business at NETWORK6 last night — let's stay in touch")
- Send a brief follow-up email to anyone where there was a genuine business conversation: "It was good to meet you last night. I mentioned I could send you a quick demo of what [their type of business] could look like on our website — here's the link. No obligation, just interesting to see."

**Within 1 week:**
- If someone expressed genuine interest, schedule a call or coffee — do not let it go cold. "Following up on our conversation — I've put together a quick preview of what [their business] could look like. Happy to jump on a 15-minute call to walk through it?"

**Ongoing:**
- Engage with their LinkedIn content (like, comment meaningfully) so you stay on their radar without being pushy
- If you see something relevant to their business — a new tool, a grant announcement, a relevant blog post — forward it with a brief note. "Saw this and thought of our conversation at NETWORK6 — there's a SOSE grant that might cover part of a website project for businesses like yours."

### Building the Referral Network (Long-Term Play)

The most valuable outcome from D&G networking is not client acquisition — it is referral relationships. Target these specifically:

- **Accountants** (Bell Ogilvy, Rennie Accountants, etc.) — they are trusted advisers to every SME in their portfolio. One accountant who refers clients to Nith Digital is worth 20 cold calls.
- **Business Gateway advisers** — their job is to point clients toward support. Being known = being recommended.
- **SOSE advisers** — same dynamic, different funding route.
- **Solicitors** — especially commercial property solicitors who handle business sales, leases, and new business formations. Every new business formation is a potential first website client.
- **Print and signage companies** — complementary but non-competing. A signage company client often needs a matching website. A website client often needs signage. Formal referral arrangement possible: 10% of referred project value.
