## How These URLs Work

Each URL opens the relevant Nith Digital template demo with the prospect's name, location, phone, and email pre-populated as query parameters. When you send one of these to a prospect, or open it in a meeting, they see a version of the template that already looks like *their* business — name on the header, their town in the footer, their contact details on the contact page.

**Usage tips:**
- Paste the URL into an email as a plain hyperlink — don't use URL shorteners
- In a cold email, say: "I put together a 2-minute demo with your name on it — [link]"
- In a face-to-face pitch, open it on your laptop or phone before the meeting and hand it to them
- If a business has no website at all, this link IS the pitch

## Notes on Constructing Additional URLs

For any business not listed here, the URL structure is:

```
nithdigital.uk/templates/[template-slug]?name=[Business+Name]&location=[Town]&phone=[number]&email=[address]
```

Available template slugs:
- `highland-rest` — Hotels, B&Bs, self-catering, guest houses
- `nithsdale-plumbing` — Tradespeople: plumbers, electricians, joiners, builders, mechanics
- `castle-kitchen` — Restaurants, cafés, tearooms, pubs with food
- `galloway-harvest` — Farm shops, delis, food producers, retail
- `solway-homes` — Estate agents, property managers, letting agents

Use `%26` for `&` in business names, `%2C` for commas, `+` for spaces.
