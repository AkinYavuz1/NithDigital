D&G is a large, sparsely populated rural region spanning the south-west corner of Scotland. Its dispersed geography means businesses in smaller towns (Kirkcudbright, Gatehouse of Fleet, Portpatrick, Wigtown) have limited local footfall and depend heavily on their online presence to draw visitors from outside the region.

## 2. Business Landscape

### Total Business Count
- **6,295 businesses** in Dumfries and Galloway (2024/25 data)
- 88.3% are micro businesses (0–9 employees)
- 10.5% are small businesses (10–49 employees)
- 1.0% are medium businesses (50–249)
- 0.2% are large businesses (250+)

**Active registered companies (Companies House):** ~4,294
**New companies (past 2 years):** 812
**Total employees across all businesses:** 25,033

### Sector Breakdown (by employment dominance)
1. **Human health & social work** — largest employer
2. **Wholesale & retail trade** — second largest
3. **Construction** — third largest
4. **Agriculture & forestry** — major contributor (larger share than Scotland average)
5. **Professional activities** — growing sector
6. **Accommodation & food services** — 630 tourism enterprises = 9.9% of all businesses
7. **Education, manufacturing, transport** — remaining employment

### Key Economic Anchors
- **Food & drink sector** — annual turnover of £1.29 billion, employing 13,700 people; D&G's most valuable economic sector
- **Forestry & timber** — significant rural employer
- **Public sector** — NHS, DG Council, education; large proportion of total employment
- **Tourism** — £158 million annual turnover (2023), 7,000 employed (11.9% of all employment)
- **Agriculture** — strong beef, lamb, dairy (Loch Arthur, Barnbarroch Organic), and arable

### Tourism Businesses
- **630 tourism enterprises** = 9.9% of all businesses in D&G
- Includes hotels, B&Bs, self-catering, activities, attractions, food & drink

### Key Tourism Assets
- **Galloway Forest Park** — UK's first International Dark Sky Park; 7stanes mountain bike trails
- **Robert Burns Heritage** — major draw (Dumfries is Burns' final home)
- **Wigtown** — Scotland's National Book Town; Wigtown Book Festival (annual, 10 days, major literary event)
- **Solway Coast** — AONB, Caerlaverock Castle, wetlands
- **Golf** — multiple courses including Portpatrick Dunskey, Stranraer, Powfoot
- **Cycling** — National Cycling Routes, C2C route
- **Dumfries & Galloway** hosts the annual Wickerman Festival and Scotland's largest agriculture show (DG Galloway County Show)

### Regional Tourism Growth Strategy
The South of Scotland Responsible Tourism Strategy targets:
- Growing tourism to **£1.76 billion** economic value
- **20,000 full-time equivalent jobs** by 2034
- 20% growth in visitor spend in 2022–2023 alone (£150m increase)

## 4. Key Economic Challenges Relevant to Nith Digital

1. **Rural digital divide** — 34% of Scottish SME employers are rural-based; rural businesses show lower broadband adoption rates and digital investment vs urban counterparts (Scottish Government Digital Economy Report 2024)
2. **Ageing business owner demographic** — 34.73% of company directors in D&G are over 60; many built businesses before websites were expected and are resistant to change unless shown clear ROI
3. **Micro-business dominance** — 88% are micro; most lack in-house marketing resource and rely on word of mouth or legacy directories
4. **Tourism dependency** — with 9.9% of all businesses in hospitality/tourism, seasonal trading pressures make online booking and year-round visibility critical
5. **Distance from cities** — no regional newspaper has strong digital presence; local SEO is underserved and relatively easier to dominate than urban markets

## 6. Digital Funding Available to D&G Businesses

### DigitalBoost Development Grant (currently paused)
- Was: 50/50 matched grants of £2,500–£20,000 for digital capability
- Covered: apps, online booking, digital consultancy, IT software/hardware
- **Status as of 2024:** CLOSED (contract ended 13 May 2023, currently paused pending review)
- Still useful as a sales hook: businesses that applied and were rejected can be targeted; businesses unaware of the fund may be receptive when told funding has historically existed

### SOSE Grants
- No dedicated digital grant at present, but SOSE advisers can signpost businesses to appropriate support
- Business training and advisory grants exist for digital upskilling
- Opportunity: Nith Digital could explore SOSE "supply-side" support as a digital services provider

### Business Gateway Digital Health Check
- Free tool for any Scottish business; identifies digital gaps
- Businesses completing the check often receive recommendations to improve website, SEO, and e-commerce
- Nith Digital opportunity: partner with BG as a recommended agency post-check

## 7. Digital Adoption Stats — Scottish Rural Businesses

- **35% of Scottish SME employers are rural-based** (Small Business Survey Scotland 2024–25)
- Rural businesses have demonstrably lower rates of digital adoption than urban counterparts
- Scottish Government's 2021–2024 Digital Economy Progress Report confirmed "significant strides" in urban digital economy but highlighted "ongoing rural digital divide"
- In Dumfries & Galloway specifically, a high proportion of hospitality businesses (B&Bs, holiday cottages, activity operators) still rely on third-party platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, Tripadvisor) rather than their own websites — paying 15–25% commission per booking
- Many tradespeople in D&G have no website at all or use legacy sites built in the early 2010s that are not mobile-responsive
