## The Problem With Facebook for Trade Businesses

Let's be clear: Facebook isn't useless. A trade page on Facebook can help. But treating it as your main — or only — digital presence is a genuine business risk. Here's why.

**Facebook's organic reach is gone.** When you post on your business page, only 2–5% of your followers see it. That's not a typo. Facebook throttles business page content intentionally to push you towards paid advertising. The customers you've worked hard to get as followers simply won't see your posts unless you pay.

**Facebook has no SEO.** When someone in Dumfries searches "plumber Dumfries" on Google at 9pm on a Sunday because their boiler has packed in, your Facebook page will not appear. Google doesn't rank Facebook business pages for local search queries. It ranks websites. If you're not on Google, you're not in that conversation.

**You don't own it.** Everything you've built on Facebook — your photos, your reviews, your followers — belongs to Meta. Your account can be restricted, reported, hacked, or caught in a technical glitch and you lose everything. No appeal process, no recourse. We've seen it happen to local businesses here in D&G, and it's devastating.

**It doesn't look as credible as a website.** This is uncomfortable to say, but it's true. When a homeowner is trying to decide between two tradespeople they've never met, and one has a proper website with their services listed, photos of their work, clear pricing, and a contact form — and the other just has a Facebook page — the one with the website wins the benefit of the doubt. Every time.

## What Customers Actually Expect Before Hiring a Tradesperson

Put yourself in your customer's shoes for a minute.

They need a new bathroom fitting, or they've got a leak, or they want their house rewired. They ask a friend for a recommendation — great, that still happens. But then what do they do? They Google the person's name to check them out. They look for reviews, photos, a sense of who they're dealing with before they invite someone into their home.

If all they find is a Facebook page, or nothing at all, they hesitate. And a hesitating customer is a customer who might call someone else.

What builds confidence for a customer before they hire a tradesperson?

- **Photos of real work** — before and after, jobs similar to what they need
- **Clear service areas** — "covering Dumfries, Sanquhar, Thornhill and surrounding areas"
- **What you actually do** — a clear list of services so they know you handle their specific job
- **How to contact you** — easy, frictionless, ideally an enquiry form as well as a phone number
- **Reviews and testimonials** — especially from local customers they might recognise
- **Qualifications and accreditations** — Gas Safe, NICEIC, CSCS, etc. — these matter enormously to homeowners

A well-built trade website gives you all of this in one place, working for you 24 hours a day.

## What a Good Trade Website Actually Needs

You don't need anything fancy. You need the right things, done well:

**1. A mobile-first design**
The majority of people searching for tradespeople are doing it on a phone. Your site needs to look and work great on a small screen first.

**2. A clear, specific service list**
Not just "plumbing services" — "boiler installation, boiler servicing, gas safety certificates, bathroom fitting, emergency callouts." Each of these is a search term someone might use.

**3. Your service area, stated clearly**
"Based in Dumfries, covering all of Dumfriesshire and Galloway" — or more specific towns. This is critical for local SEO.

**4. Photos of your actual work**
Before and after. Jobs you're proud of. It doesn't need to be professional photography — well-lit phone photos work fine. Authenticity matters more than polish.

**5. A quote request or enquiry form**
Make it easy to get in touch. Name, phone number, "what do you need?" — that's all it needs to be. Remove friction wherever possible.

**6. Your qualifications and accreditations**
Gas Safe? NICEIC registered? Part P certified? Display these prominently. They matter to customers.

**7. Google reviews, integrated or linked**
Either embed your Google reviews or link directly to your Google Business Profile. Social proof is powerful.
