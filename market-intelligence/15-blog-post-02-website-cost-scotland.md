## Option 1: DIY Website Builders (£0–£50/month)

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, Weebly, and the free tier of WordPress.com let anyone build a basic website without technical knowledge.

**What you get:**
- A functional site, usually up within a weekend
- Drag-and-drop templates — no coding required
- Hosting included in the subscription
- Basic e-commerce (on paid plans)

**What you don't get:**
- Full control over your site's code or performance
- Strong SEO out of the box (Wix in particular has a long history of SEO limitations)
- Anything that looks truly distinctive — you're using the same templates as thousands of other businesses
- Easy migration if you ever want to move your site to a different platform

**Typical monthly cost:**
- Free tier: £0 (but includes ads and no custom domain)
- Basic paid plans: £10–£20/month
- Business/e-commerce plans: £25–£50/month

Over three years, a paid Wix or Squarespace plan costs £360–£1,800. It's not actually "free" — it's a rental model.

**Who it's right for:** A sole trader who just needs an online presence to share a link, someone testing an idea before committing budget, or a very simple business with no SEO ambitions.

## Option 2: Template Websites — Built for You (£200–£500 one-off + hosting)

A small step up: a designer takes a premium template, customises it with your branding, content, and images, and hands it over. Some agencies offer these as productised packages.

**What you get:**
- A professionally set-up site, not a DIY job
- Your branding, logo, colours applied properly
- Basic on-page SEO set up correctly
- Usually 3–5 pages: home, about, services, contact
- Often a Content Management System (CMS) so you can update it yourself

**What you don't get:**
- Bespoke design — it's still a template
- Complex functionality (booking, e-commerce, custom forms)
- In-depth SEO strategy or ongoing optimisation

**Typical cost:**
- One-off build: £200–£500
- Annual hosting/maintenance: £100–£250/year

## Option 3: Custom-Built Website (£1,000–£5,000)

This is where the work is tailored specifically to your business — custom design, bespoke functionality, proper SEO structure, and built to grow with you.

**What you get:**
- Design that reflects your specific brand, not a template
- Pages structured specifically for your business and services
- Local SEO built into the architecture from the start
- Custom functionality — booking forms, quote calculators, contact workflows, e-commerce
- Better performance (faster loading, cleaner code) than DIY platforms
- Ongoing support options

**What affects the price in this range:**
- Number of pages
- Whether you need e-commerce
- Whether you need booking/calendar functionality
- Quality of photography (professional photos add cost but are worth it)
- Whether ongoing SEO or content work is included

## Option 4: Large Agency Website (£5,000–£20,000+)

At the top end are full-service digital agencies — typically based in Edinburgh, Glasgow, or London — that charge for large teams, project management overhead, brand strategy, and premium custom development.

**What you get:**
- Full brand strategy and design system
- Fully custom development (often React/Next.js or similar)
- Dedicated account management
- Detailed analytics setup
- Large-scale e-commerce or web application capability

**What you should know:**
- For most small businesses in Scotland, this level is overkill
- A lot of what you pay for at this tier is overhead, not your website
- You're often working with a junior team despite senior pitches
- Ongoing costs (retainers, hosting, updates) can be £500–£2,000/month

**Who it's right for:** Large hospitality groups, multi-branch businesses, companies needing truly bespoke applications, or businesses with a marketing budget to match.

## What Actually Affects the Price?

Beyond the tier you're in, specific factors push costs up or down:

**Goes up:**
- E-commerce / online shop functionality
- Booking or reservation systems
- Multiple languages
- Integration with external systems (CRM, accounting software, booking platforms)
- Large number of pages or products
- Professional photography or video included in the package
- Ongoing SEO or content writing

**Comes down:**
- You provide all content and photos
- Fewer pages
- Template-based rather than fully custom
- You don't need e-commerce or booking
- Quick turnaround isn't required

## The Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

Whatever route you choose, make sure you understand the ongoing costs:

- **Domain name:** £10–£15/year (you should own this yourself, not the agency)
- **Hosting:** £5–£30/month depending on quality
- **SSL certificate:** usually included in hosting
- **Maintenance/updates:** either a monthly retainer (£50–£200) or pay-as-you-go
- **Content updates:** who updates the site, and what does it cost?
- **SEO/ongoing marketing:** a site with no ongoing work will gradually slip in rankings

A good agency will be upfront about all of these from day one. If they're not, ask.
